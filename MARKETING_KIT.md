## 7. First-week outreach plan

A realistic Week 1 checklist. Pace yourself — consistent daily activity beats a single viral push.

### Day 1 (Monday)
- [ ] Publish landing page live
- [ ] Post Variation A to 2 Facebook teacher groups
- [ ] Post WhatsApp intro in 3 teacher groups you're already in
- [ ] Post Twitter thread
- [ ] Message 5 teacher friends directly

### Day 2 (Tuesday)
- [ ] Post Variation B to 2 different Facebook groups
- [ ] Send "forward me" WhatsApp post to any parent + teacher contacts
- [ ] Reply to every comment from Day 1 — tone = warm, specific, helpful

### Day 3 (Wednesday)
- [ ] Email first TTC (Kitwe TTC is usually most responsive)
- [ ] Email 3 private schools in Lusaka
- [ ] Check waitlist signups → reply personally to every one

### Day 4 (Thursday)
- [ ] Post Variation C (beta call) to 2 Facebook groups
- [ ] Pick 3 waitlist teachers, give them manual access, ask them to generate one plan and tell you what's wrong

### Day 5 (Friday)
- [ ] Email second TTC (David Livingstone or Mufulira)
- [ ] Email 3 private schools in Kitwe or Ndola
- [ ] Write a "what I learned this week" WhatsApp post with one or two specific things teachers asked for, post it to the groups where you posted Day 1-3

### Weekend
- [ ] Rest. Don't post — teacher WhatsApp groups are quiet on weekends and you want Monday reach.
- [ ] Review waitlist data: which grades, which subjects, which provinces? That tells you what to build next.

### By end of Week 1 you should have
- [ ] 50-100 waitlist signups
- [ ] 5-10 teachers who have actually tried the tool and given feedback
- [ ] 1-2 TTC replies
- [ ] 1-2 head-teacher meetings scheduled

If you don't hit those numbers, the bottleneck is either the channel (wrong WhatsApp groups) or the pitch (landing page isn't converting). Iterate on whichever is weaker.

## 8. What to say when things go wrong

### "The AI is wrong about this topic"
> Thank you — that kind of feedback is exactly what founding teachers help with. Could you tell me (a) the grade, (b) the subject, (c) the topic, and (d) what specifically was wrong? I'll get it fixed this week.

### "K89 is too expensive"
> Understood. The free tier (10 lesson plans, 5 worksheets, 20 flashcards every month) covers a two-week planning cycle for most teachers, and it's free forever. If you genuinely need more, you can also pay yearly (K799, save K269). Let me know if that still doesn't work — I'm building for Zambian teachers and pricing needs to match what you actually earn.

### "Does it support [Bemba / Nyanja / Tonga]?"
> Not yet — English today. Local language support is on the roadmap for next year. Would you use it in Bemba or Nyanja specifically if it did? That tells me where to prioritise.

### "Can I use this for [Grade 11 / Grade 12]?"
> Yes, it works for Grades 10-12, but the syllabus grounding is stronger for Grades 1-9 right now. You'll see a small yellow note on the output saying "used general CBC knowledge" for Grades 10-12 — it means the plan is fine but review carefully. We're adding senior-secondary topics week by week.

## 9. Metrics to track from Day 1

Export these weekly into a Google Sheet. Don't skip.

- Landing page visitors (Netlify Analytics or Plausible)
- Landing → waitlist conversion rate (target ≥8%)
- Waitlist → active signup (target ≥60%)
- Signup → first generation (target ≥70% within 24h)
- Free → paid conversion at 30 days (target ≥4%)
- Cost per paid acquisition — (AI costs + your time) / paid teachers
- NPS — ask once, at the end of week 2, single question: "How likely are you to recommend ZedExams Teacher Suite to another Zambian teacher?"
