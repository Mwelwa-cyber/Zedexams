The Lungs and Breathing ~120 words · Grade 4 readability
Our lungs are very important organs in the body. They are part of the respiratory system. When we breathe in, air enters through the nose and passes down a tube called the trachea. The trachea divides into two smaller tubes called bronchi, which carry air into the two lungs.

Inside the lungs, oxygen from the air passes into the blood. The blood then carries the oxygen to every part of the body. At the same time, the lungs help remove a waste gas called carbon dioxide from the body. Without our lungs, we cannot live.
